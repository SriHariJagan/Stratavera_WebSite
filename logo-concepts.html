Concept 1 · Stratum

Ascending strata

The idea: five horizontal bars, each shifted up and to the right, forming an ascending staircase. Reads simultaneously as geological strata (the sediment of accumulated experience) and as strategic ascent (the work moving forward through layers). Most visually distinctive of the four — the silhouette is unique enough to be recognised at a glance.

Tone: confident, modern, momentum-forward. Best for an Indian/emerging-markets audience that wants a firm that feels current.

Concept 2 · Apex

Strategic peak

The idea: five centred horizontal bars of decreasing length, building from a wide foundation to a single point. Reads literally as strategy emerging from depth — every layer of the foundation contributes to the apex. The shape also evokes mountain, achievement and direction.

Tone: classical, considered, slightly more conservative. Strong fit if the firm wants to telegraph stability and seniority above novelty.

Concept 3 · Column

Stratified pillar

The idea: a vertical pillar made of stacked horizontal strata of alternating widths, capped by a base and capital. Reads as architectural cross-section — directly linking to the infrastructure sector — and also as fluted classical column (stability, institutional weight). The most explicitly "infrastructure" of the four marks.

Tone: the most institutional / authoritative. Natural fit if Stratavera will work primarily with sovereign clients, multilaterals and large developers where gravitas matters more than energy.

Concept 4 · Wordmark

No mark, type-led

The idea: no symbol at all. The wordmark is the brand — STRATAVERA in a confident sans, with PARTNERS sitting tighter beneath in spaced caps. A small three-line stratified rule between the two words is the only graphic device, and it nods to the strata theme without competing with the type.

Tone: most institutional and most flexible — works at any size, in any context, including in-line in a sentence. Reads like McKinsey, BCG, Oliver Wyman. Best if Stratavera intends to compete on substance and let the name do the work.
